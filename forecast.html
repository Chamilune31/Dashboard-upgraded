Forecast vs Actual

Compare what really happened (the actual sales) with what the system predicted (the forecast) - for any item you choose. The same item is shown in two ways: against the actual line, and across two consecutive snapshots, so you can see exactly how the forecast moved.

How to read this page - order of inspection

1
Pick an item from the dropdown (top suspicious items are pre-loaded so you can start immediately).
2
Look at the left chart first. If the orange "actual" line stays inside the blue "forecast" band, the forecast is healthy. If it escapes the band, you need to understand why.
3
Then look at the right chart. Two coloured lines = two snapshots. Where the lines disagree, the forecast was revised between the two runs.
4
Apply the checklist below to decide whether to validate or push the forecast back.

2Actual sales vs forecast

In plain words Orange line = real sales. Blue line = forecast. Light-blue band around the forecast = "normal" range (plus or minus 1 standard deviation of past revisions).
Read it like this: if the orange line stays inside the band, your forecast is in good shape. If it pokes out, that month's forecast probably needs a manual review.

3Snapshot 1 vs Snapshot 2 - overlap

In plain words Two coloured lines = two consecutive forecast runs for the same item. If the lines sit on top of each other, the forecast didn't change much between runs (good). If they diverge, the planning system materially changed its mind - that's worth understanding.

4Demand-planner checklist

Decision rules
  1. Actual outside the band -> upstream sell-in correction or sudden demand shift. Confirm with the sales team.
  2. Actual diverges then snaps back -> likely a one-off promotion or event. Probably safe.
  3. Snapshot 2 is higher than Snapshot 1 -> forecast was revised up. Confirm new actuals support the increase.
  4. Snapshot 2 is lower than Snapshot 1 -> forecast was revised down. Verify with sell-out and current inventory.
