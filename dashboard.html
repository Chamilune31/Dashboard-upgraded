Forecast Health Overview

A single page to check whether the forecasts can be validated, or whether some items / months need a manual review first.

ⓘ
Sampling notice - values are based on a 2 000 000-row sample.
The full dataset contains approximately 97 M rows. To keep the dashboard responsive, a representative subset of 2 M rows was loaded into memory. The sampling was not performed by drawing random individual rows; instead, a selection of items was identified and every row belonging to those items was retained in full. This preserves the complete time-series history for each sampled item (all snapshot months, all forecast horizons), which is required for the revision-detection and anomaly-scoring models to work correctly. Items outside the selection are not represented in this view.

How to read this dashboard - a 7-step walkthrough

1
Start with the colored tiles below. They give you the headline numbers in one glance: how many items were revised, how many rows look inconsistent, and how many were flagged by each detector. If the warning-coloured tiles look high, something needs review.
2
Check the monthly trend chart. It shows when the problems happened. Spikes on the orange or red lines tell you which months to investigate first.
3
Look at the revision-size histogram. It shows how big the forecast changes actually were. A distribution skewed to large values means revisions aren't just frequent — they're large.
4
Look at the country bar chart. Countries on the left have the most inconsistencies - focus your audit there before everywhere else.
5
Scan the Top-10 most-revised items table. These products had the biggest average forecast changes between snapshots.
6
Then check the Top-10 suspicious items. This blends all three detectors (CI, Isolation Forest, Logistic Regression) into one composite ranking — these are your highest-priority items to review.
7
Click "Inspect" on a suspicious item to open its detail page, with the full time-series, anomaly markers, and snapshot overlay. That's where you decide: validate the forecast, or push it back?
